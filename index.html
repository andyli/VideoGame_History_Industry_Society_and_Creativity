Assessments

Short reports

20% (5% each) of total grade.

Short report 1

Analyze the card game "Go Fish!" based on the formal game elements that were introduced in lecture 2:

  • Players
  • Objective
  • Procedures
  • Rules
  • Conflict
  • Boundaries
  • Outcome

You should introduce and analyze the game in your own words (no copy and paste from web sites). Be sure to describe all the above game elements in the game.

The report should be less than 1000 words, typed in English in complete sentences.

Include your name, student id (5xxxxxxx) in the header of the report.

Short report 2

Create a new game by adding a story to "Go Fish!".

For story, it can be a simple story that features characters going on a quest, or just an action (e.g. hunting). The objective here is to add meaning to the game elements such that the gameplay is concrete, logical and easy to understand. You should explicitly state what the game elements are in the story (e.g. players are hunters, and the pool of cards is the forest which the cards are monsters etc.).

You may alter the game in any way you want to better fit the story. For example, you may assign roles to the players that each of them has different rules to follow. You are encouraged to design graphics for the cards.

In the report, introduce the game, including the name, the gameplay and the story. You should assume the reader doesn't know "Go Fish!". It should be less than 1500 words, typed in English in complete sentences. Use pictures to illustrate if needed (state all media sources).

Include your name, student id (5xxxxxxx) in the header of the report.

Submit the report in pdf format to my email before the deadline:
2014-02-19 23:59:59

Late submission will result in a one letter grade deduction (e.g. from A- to B-). Submission received 1 week after the deadline will not be graded.

Short report 4

Draft a design of an educational video game. The player experience goal of the game is to learn or master a subject in a playful and creative way. The subject can be acadamic related (e.g. periodic table) or not (e.g. how to use chopsticks). You should mention the target platform (e.g. PC/XBox/3DS/arcade machine...) and the required hardware (e.g. keyboard/webcam/game pad...).

There is no need to design every detail of the game, but the report should include a brief overview of game rules, game mechanics and any feature that you think is interesting or significant. Design at least one "screenshot" of the gameplay. Remember to label the source of the images which are taken from the Internet, at the footer or in a "bibliography"/"reference" section at the end of your report.

The report should be less than 1500 words, typed in English in complete sentences.

Final project

50% of total grade.

5-7 students a group (same groups as presentation).

Each group will produce a game. The game can either be a video game or a board/card game.

The game should be about a Hong Kong local topic. Some good topics are:

  • Bun snatching (搶包山)
  • Milk powder shortage (搶購奶粉)
  • Free-TV license (免費視牌照)
  • Caged Home (籠屋)
  • Cha chaan teng (茶餐廳)
  • Li's Field (李氏力場)
  • etc.

The game should be produced as if it is going to be released to the market. That is, unless it is an online/mobile game, it should be contained by a game box, together with game instruction, and all necessary elements (may be game board, cards, installation dics.). In the case of video game, the game should be complete and playable on a modern computer with common input device. You may use any commerial or free library, game engine or level editor.

Record all design process. It includes: Take photos of game prototypes; write down comments and review of play test sections; list out source of any media reference/style guide.

You will present the game during the last lesson: 2014-04-17

Presentation slides (with optional seperated report, if the slides does not contain everything) should be submitted to me via email in pdf format, within 1 week after the presentation.

Late submission will result in a one letter grade deduction (e.g. from A- to B-). Submission received 1 week after the deadline will not be graded.
